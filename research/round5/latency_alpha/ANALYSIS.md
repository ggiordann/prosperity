The trader uses all 50 products. It keeps the validated same-category lead-lag core as the executable strategy because the all-pairs cross-category scanner found many statistically attractive 50-tick relationships, but the broad walk-forward cross-category set was negative after spread and was more unstable than the category-specific overlays.

Exact lag set used in the final trader: `1`, `2`, `5`, `10`, `20`, `50`, `78`, `100`, `200`, and `500` ticks across 96 retained lag overlays. The required 1/2/3/5/10/20/50-tick all-pairs scan is below; the longer 78/100/200/500 lags are retained from the prior robust category search where they improved realized Rust PnL.

Final product/category PnL tables are saved beside this report:

- `research/round5/latency_alpha/final_product_pnl.csv`
- `research/round5/latency_alpha/final_category_pnl.csv`

Generated by `research/round5/latency_alpha/latency_scanner.py` from public Round 5 days +2, +3, and +4.

The scan covers all 50 products and every ordered product pair over leader-return horizons 1, 2, 3, 5, 10, 20, and 50 ticks against follower future-return horizons 1, 2, 3, 5, 10, 20, and 50 ticks. Event PnL columns mark the follower at future mid and subtract the full current spread for `event_net_cross_ticks`, so positive values are deliberately conservative.
